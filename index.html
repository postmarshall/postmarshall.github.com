Handling Email as a Team

If your team is relying on email to communicate with customers you have probably noticed email is a crappy tool to commucate as a team. You’re probable familiar with the following scenario:

  • A customer sends a mail to your team with a question.
  • Nick (one of your team members) replies to your customers email. Unless Nick is an email ninja he will be using his own email address. Nick’s reply will be stored in Nick’s Sent items folder.
  • The next day that same customers replies to Nick’s message. This message will go to Nicks inbox. Too bad Nick has his day off. Now you’re customer’s email won’t be answered till after the weekend.

Scenario’s like this are caused by a lack of good tools to handle that flood of email your team has to handle daily. This is because most email systems are designed with individuals in mind, not teams.

  • Usually email is delivered to someone’s personal inbox instead of your team’s central mailbox
  • Some systems already have Public folders, which can be used as a team’s mailbox, but:
    • When you reply to a mail in a Public folder the from and reply-to address on your reply email will contain your personal address instead of the team’s address. This will move the conversation to your personal inbox, where your team cannot help you.
    • Your reply is stored in your Sent Items, where nobody of your team can reach it. This way none of your team members are aware of your reply.
    • You need IT staff to manage your mailboxes

These kind of scenarios could be avoided with the right tools. Enter PostMarshall:

  • PostMarshall has true team mailboxes:
    • Email delivered to a team’s mailbox.
    • Replies to these mails will be stored in the team’s mailbox. This way the complete conversation can be viewed by the team.
    • Replies will get the team’s email address as a reply-to address. This way the conversation stays in your team’s mailbox giving your team a complete overview of the conversation.
  • No IT staff required.
  • Budget friendly solutions (no upfront investments required).
